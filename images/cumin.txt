Cumin (Cuminum cyminum), pronounced /ˈkʌmɪn/ and sometimes spelled cummin, is a flowering plant native to the east Mediterranean to East India. Revered for its distinctive warm and earthy flavor, cumin seeds are a culinary treasure in various cultures. Used abundantly in Indian and Pakistani cuisines, cumin adds a robust and aromatic touch to a myriad of dishes. The seeds of the cumin plant are the edible part and are known for their essential role in spice blends, curry powders, and as a standalone spice.
